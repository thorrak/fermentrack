iSpindel Guided Calibration

Overview

Although iSpindel devices are used to measure the specific gravity of a solution during fermentation, the device actually measures the angle of tilt rather than the specific gravity directly. To translate between the two we must come up with a formula to convert angle to SG. Unfortunately for us, due to its DIY nature, every iSpindel device must be calibrated individually. That said, with a little bit of work it's not difficult to calibrate the device.

This process will guide you through creating sugar/water mixtures of known specific gravity and then seeing what angle the iSpindel device measures in each. By the end of this process you will have collected 8 data points which Fermentrack will then use to derive your iSpindel's conversion function. You will need a few pieces of equipment to complete this process - all of which are listed out below.

Supplies

In order to perform this guided calibration, you will need the following supplies:

  • 4 lbs (2 kg) White Table Sugar
  • Water
  • 2 cup (500 ml) measuring cup (for mixing)
  • 2+ gallon (or 6+ liter) bucket (does not need to be sanitized)
  • iSpindel Device
  • Gram scale
